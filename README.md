# India's Heatwave Crisis (2011–2021)
### A Policy Analytics Study

This project examines the patterns of heatwaves across Indian states and their relationship with heatwave-related mortality. Using publicly available government datasets from data.gov.in, the analysis applies data visualization and statistical techniques to derive evidence-based policy preparedness.

## Key Findings

The analysis reveals significant geographical variation in heatwave exposure across India. Certain states consistently record higher numbers of heatwave days, while mortality trends indicate that extreme heat continues to pose a serious public health concern. These findings highlight the need for targeted heatwave adaptation policies rather than uniform national responses.

## Conclusion

This study demonstrates how data analytics can support evidence-based policymaking by identifying patterns of heatwaves and public health vulnerability. By combining statistical analysis and visualisations, the project highlights the importance of translating data into accessible insights that inform policy decisions.

## Policy Recommendations

Based on the findings, the policymakers should prioritise strengthening state-specific Heat Action Plans in high exposure regions. There is a need to expand early warning systems, improve public awareness campaigns and integrate heatwave preparedness into urban planning and public health strategies. Long term climate adaptation policies should focus on protecting vulnerable populations through data driven resource allocation and continuous monitoring of heatwave trends.
